About Follistatin

Follistatin is a secreted glycoprotein that has earned major scientific attention because of its high-affinity binding to several members of the TGF-beta superfamily, especially activins and myostatin. Through these interactions, it is recognized as an important regulator of muscle biology, cellular differentiation, and signal-balancing processes in complex biological systems. [web:149][web:150][web:141]

In molecular and preclinical research, follistatin is widely studied for its ability to inhibit pathways that normally restrain skeletal muscle growth. This has made it a central molecule in investigations involving myostatin blockade, muscle hypertrophy, tissue remodeling, and the broader regulation of protein signaling networks linked to development and repair. [web:141][web:142][web:147]

Scientific interest in follistatin extends beyond muscle biology alone. Published research also describes its relevance to bone metabolism, vascularization, regeneration, and other cellular environments influenced by activin and related ligands, which is why it remains a highly referenced molecule in modern biomedical research.
