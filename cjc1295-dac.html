About CJC-1295 DAC

CJC-1295 DAC is a long-acting synthetic analog of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) that has become well known in endocrine and peptide research because its Drug Affinity Complex enables extended activity compared with shorter-acting GHRH analogs. Its design allows binding to circulating serum albumin, which markedly prolongs its presence in the bloodstream. [web:110][web:119][web:121]

In research settings, CJC-1295 DAC is studied for its ability to sustain growth hormone signaling over longer periods than non-DAC forms. Published human data reported dose-dependent increases in growth hormone for 6 days or more and increases in IGF-1 lasting roughly 9 to 11 days after a single injection, with an estimated half-life of about 5.8 to 8.1 days. [web:110]

Because of these pharmacokinetic properties, CJC-1295 DAC is widely referenced in scientific discussions involving pituitary function, GH axis regulation, and peptide-based endocrine modulation. It is particularly relevant in studies that compare short-acting and long-acting GHRH analog strategies and explore how prolonged signaling may influence downstream hormonal responses. [web:110][web:112][web:120]
